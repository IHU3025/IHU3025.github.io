Part 1: Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Perspective distortion occurs due to the exaggerated perspective effect when using a wide-angle lens up close. When the object is closer to the phone, the camera uses a short focal length with a large Field of View. Since the object distance is small, the relative distance from the lens to the closest feature vs. the furthest feature is large. This results in closer features being rendered disproportionately larger than further ones, creating perspective distortion.

On the other hand, when you zoom in, the camera adjusts to a telephoto or portrait lens setting. By stepping back and zooming in to keep the face the same size in the frame, the relative distance difference becomes smaller, and facial features don't have this exaggerated effect.
